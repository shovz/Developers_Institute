Early Life

Stephen Robert Irwin was born on 22 February 1962
in Upper Ferntree Gully, Victoria, Australia.
He moved with his parents as a child to
Queensland in 1970,where he attended Landsborough
State School and Caloundra State High School.

Irwin described his father Bob as a wildlife expert
interested in herpetology, while his mother Lyn was a
wildlife rehabilitator.After moving to Queensland,
Bob and Lyn Irwin started the small
Queensland Reptile and Fauna Park, where Steve
grew up around crocodiles and other reptiles.

Irwin became involved with the park in a number of
ways, including taking part in daily animal feeding, as well as care and maintenance activities.
On his sixth birthday, he was given a 4m scrub python.
He began handling crocodiles and even wrestled his
first crocodile at the age of nine after his father
had educated him on reptiles from an early age.

He worked as a volunteer for Queensland's East Coast
Crocodile Management program and captured over 100
crocodiles, some of which were relocated, while
others were housed at the family park. Irwin took
over the management of the park in 1991 and renamed
it to Australia Zoo in 1998
